U.S. September Inflation Lower Than Expected, Stocks Rise

The U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) reported an increase in the year-on-year Consumer Price Index (CPI) with goods on average costing 3.0% more in September 2025 compared to September 2024. This is an increase from the 2.9% CPI in August. Gasoline prices contributed significantly, rising 4.1% over the month.

In addition to sector-specific changes, economists believe the imposition of tariffs has caused rising costs across various categories, adding to overall inflation. Current estimations on the impact of the tariffs suggest an approximate 0.4 percentage point addition to inflation.

Despite the increase, it remains below analyst expectations, contributing to gains in U.S. stocks. Major stock indices closed at record highs with the Dow Jones (DJIA) closing above 47,000 for the first time.

Although inflation still remains above the 2% target of the Federal Reserve, it is not accelerating as quickly as expected.

Nonfarm payroll employment increased by 22,000 jobs in August, which is significantly lower than the 79,000 in July and a forecasted 80,000 for August. Analysts believe the latest inflation data coupled with weakened job growth could justify potential future interest rate cuts in order to increase employment.
