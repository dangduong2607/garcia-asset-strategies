Understanding BLOOM: MAS' Newest Blockchain Initiative

ANALYSIS

On 16 October 2025, the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) announced an initiative known as Borderless, Liquid, Open, Online Multi-currency (BLOOM).

BLOOM is a digital settlement network that allows financial institutions to move money between them in tokenised form. In simple terms, it is a digital way for financial institutions to process payments in a faster and cheaper way by running on the blockchain.

As of time of writing, 16 financial institutions including J.P. Morgan, DBS and Temasek are already members of the BLOOM initiative.

MAS lists 3 focus areas for BLOOM:

  • Distribution and clearing of settlement assets
  • Programmable controls to enhance and automate compliance checks
  • Agentic payments for seamless and automated transactions

The following is a hypothetical example illustrating how BLOOM's focus areas could potentially work in a real-world trade finance scenario.

How might BLOOM work?: Trade Finance Scenario

Let's say a Singapore retailer wants to import laptops from a Japanese manufacturer. The deal is priced at ¥50 million. Under a traditional trade finance framework, the retailer's bank (Bank R) would issue a letter of credit to the manufacturer's bank (Bank M).

Shipping documents to confirm goods have been shipped are forwarded to Bank R for verification. Upon confirmation Bank R manually releases payment to Bank M, with compliance checks carried out by both banks.

However, digital settlement networks like BLOOM could potentially allow Bank R to create a programmable tokenised letter of credit. Bank R could instead programme the release of the ¥50 million upon verification of the automated checks.

BLOOM's programmable controls help conduct compliance checks through "standardised mechanisms" which ensures consistent implementation and reduced costs through automation.

BLOOM is also integrated with AI agents that automatically conduct the payments within set conditions. BLOOM also supports multiple currencies such as G10 and Asian currencies. This reduces the manual burden traditionally required to process the payment.

Traditional Trade Finance Digital Settlement Trade Finance
Letter of Credit A letter that must be acted on by the other bank (usually manual) Automatically executes instructions
Confirmation and Compliance Checks Usually done through manual checks by the staff of each bank Based on automatic, programmable controls
Payment Usually executed manually Automatically performed through AI agents

DISCLAIMER: The above scenario is a hypothetical scenario designed to illustrate a possible use case of digital settlement solutions like BLOOM. The exact functionality in BLOOM may differ from the above scenario.

Our Opinion

What we like about BLOOM is its potential to automate various processes in finance, reduce compliance costs and errors while facilitating faster, multi-currency settlements.

However, as a new platform, its adoption and real-world impact depends on how quickly financial institutions integrate it into their operations and whether its promised features perform as expected.

Implementing new technologies often requires traditional processes to change, which can be hard to integrate across many financial institutions.

If widely integrated, BLOOM could provide several benefits to enhance financial transactions. However, this will only become more clear as more institutions actively participate.
